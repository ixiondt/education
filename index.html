Our approach

This is an early-childhood educational platform grounded in the Norwegian Rammeplan — the national framework that governs pre-school education for children aged 1–6 in Norway. It is not a "drill the alphabet" app dressed up as a game; it is a deliberate, research-grounded learning tool that happens to look like one.

Four pillars

  • Omsorg (Care) — emotional safety comes before any learning outcome.
  • Lek (Play) — play is the primary mode of learning, not a reward for finishing work.
  • Læring (Learning) — emerges through play and care, never drilled.
  • Danning (Cultivation) — the holistic formation of a curious, agentic person.

What we refuse to do

  • No standardized testing for under-6s
  • No leaderboards, badges, or "level up" effects
  • No punishing streaks ("you broke your streak!")
  • No comparison to other children or developmental timelines
  • No formal reading instruction (a Grade 1 task)
  • No performance anxiety as a teaching tool

The 7 Rammeplan learning areas

A digital tool can address only some of these. Your child still needs nature, embodied play, peers, food, art, and stories — none of which this screen can give them.

  • ✅ Communication, language & text — letters, sounds, words, tracing
  • ✅ Quantities, spaces & shapes — numbers, counting, number tracing
  • ⚠️ Art, culture & creativity — indirectly, through Free play
  • ⚠️ Nature, environment & technology — animal/plant picture words
  • ⚠️ Local community & society — home/everyday picture words
  • ❌ Body, movement, food & health — outside digital scope
  • ❌ Ethics, religion & philosophy — outside digital scope

Why our design feels different

  • Wrong taps wiggle gently; no buzzer, no red X
  • Hint phrasing rotates so it never feels parrot-like
  • "Streak" counts play days, not days of correct answers
  • Mastery is hidden from the child; only parents see it
  • Achievement language is "Confident / Exploring / Suggested at..." — never "failed" or "behind"
  • "Child picks" mode lets the kid choose their target (Rammeplan §5)
  • Interest tracking — what your child gravitates toward shapes what appears next

Standards alignment

Every activity simultaneously satisfies codes from four international curricula:

  • Norwegian Rammeplan (Udir Framework Plan)
  • EU Quality Framework for ECEC (Council of EU, 2019)
  • CCSS-K (US Common Core, Kindergarten)
  • NAEYC ELOF (US National Association for the Education of Young Children)

See Progress → By standard for the per-framework dashboard.

This app is not

  • A replacement for kindergarten
  • A school-readiness drill
  • A benchmarking tool to compare your child to others
  • A literacy program
  • A defense of unlimited screen time

For the full philosophy and academic references, see the PEDAGOGY.md document that ships with the app.
